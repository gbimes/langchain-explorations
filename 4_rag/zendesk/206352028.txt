<h1>Introduction video guidelines and requirements </h1>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" dir="ltr"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><iframe src="//www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/FtQg9ltv7AM" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" dir="ltr"> </p>
<h2 class="wysiwyg-text-align-left"><strong>Guidelines:</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>You must appear in the video.</li>
<li>Your video should be between 1 to 3 minutes.</li>
<li>You must speak all the languages that you want to teach.</li>
<li>DO NOT:
<ul>
<li>Provide personal contact information</li>
<li>Advertise or promote other services</li>
<li>Use copyrighted music or any media for which you do not have the rights</li>
<li>Shoot vertical or square videos. The video aspect ratio should be 16:9 to achieve the best effect.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="wysiwyg-font-size-large">2. What should you say?</span></h3>
<p>Create a script and practice.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Who are you<br></strong>Introduce yourself. What is your name, and what languages do you speak? Your credentials, work experience, or other interesting backgrounds.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>What are your lessons like?<br></strong>What do you enjoy about teaching, do you have a preferred method, and what is your attitude towards language teaching? You might include specific details about the activities you do during lessons, show materials you use, or teach potential students their first word or phrase!</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Show your personality</strong>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Smile</strong> - Make students feel welcome to your online classes!</li>
<li>
<strong>Be yourself</strong> - Show or tell something interesting about yourself. Do you have special hobbies that students might enjoy discussing with you? Choose clothing or background that really represents you. </li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Some other tips</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Be natural</strong> - Do not read directly from a script! Look into the camera, make it as though you are speaking directly to your student.</li>
<li>
<strong>Speak in all the languages in which you are fluent</strong> - You don’t need to say the same thing again in every language, but you can move from one language to the next as you speak. We recommend adding subtitles so that students can more easily tell what you are saying in each language.</li>
<li>
<strong>Think of your students</strong> - The care you take in preparing for your video shows students not only what you think of yourself, but how they can expect you to prepare for your classes with them.</li>
<li>
<strong>Mention italki</strong> - Doing this and adding a link to your teacher profile in the description will help people find and book your classes on italki. </li>
<li>
<strong>Dress appropriately </strong>- Smart casual is acceptable, remember that 1st impressions count</li>
</ul>
