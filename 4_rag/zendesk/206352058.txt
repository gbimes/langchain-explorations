<h1>How do I become a Professional Teacher? What are the requirements for certificates?</h1>
<p>First, professional teachers must demonstrate their qualifications for teaching all the language(s) they intend to teach.</p>
<p>Examples:</p>

<h3>
<strong>Acceptable</strong><strong> </strong><strong>documents</strong>
</h3>
<p><a href="#h_01EVWYC3CAJY2C8P6DN522CZJG" target="_self" rel="undefined">1. Accredited language teaching certificate</a></p>
<p><a href="#h_01EVWYJZQ7MR77T6QJDP5Q8CF3" target="_self" rel="undefined">2. University degree in language teaching</a></p>
<p><a href="#h_01EVWYK5H5KQ7C7NAC0H5DD44M" target="_self">3. Teaching license</a></p>
<p><a href="#h_01EVWYKABWZY9119NT9E6Z8PNT" target="_self" rel="undefined">4. Proof of employment at a language teaching institution</a></p>
<p> </p>
<h4 id="h_01EVWYC3CAJY2C8P6DN522CZJG"><strong>1. Accredited language teaching certificate</strong></h4>
<p><strong>It must be issued by an accredited institution. </strong>The accreditation information is usually displayed on the certificate or available on the website of the institution.</p>
<p>Examples:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;">CELTA, DELTA (English)</span></span></li>
<li><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;">TESOL, TEFL (English, minimum of 120 hours)</span></span></li>
<li><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;">DELE, ELE (Spanish)</span></span></li>
<li>CTCSOL（国际汉语教师证书）(Chinese)          </li>
</ul>
<p> </p>
<h4 id="h_01EVWYJZQ7MR77T6QJDP5Q8CF3"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><strong>2. University degree in language teaching</strong><br></span></h4>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;">A university degree in teaching XXX as a foreign or second language (bachelor's and above).</span></p>
<p><em><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;">Degrees in literature, linguistics, or translation/interpretation don't qualify you as a professional language teacher. You need to provide other documents or proof showing your language teaching skills.</span></em> </p>
<p> </p>
<h4 id="h_01EVWYK5H5KQ7C7NAC0H5DD44M"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><strong>3. Teaching license</strong> <br></span></h4>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;"><span style="line-height: 1.45em; font-size: 1em;">A national or state teaching license for the target language.</span></span></span></p>
<p> </p>
<h4 id="h_01EVWYKABWZY9119NT9E6Z8PNT"><strong>4. Proof of employment at a language teaching institution</strong></h4>
<p>It can be a copy of a work contract showing your employment as a professional teacher or a reference letter from a school director or reputable teaching institution.</p>
<p>All documents must:</p>
<ul>
<li class="wysiwyg-text-align-left">Be signed and dated</li>
<li class="wysiwyg-text-align-left">Have clearly visible contact information</li>
<li class="wysiwyg-text-align-left">Show the dates of your employment</li>
<li class="wysiwyg-text-align-left">Describe your responsibilities as a teacher</li>
</ul>
<p><img src="https://support.italki.com/hc/article_attachments/36792926455705" alt="languag_school.jpg" width="354" height="482"></p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<h3><strong>Unacceptable documents</strong></h3>
<h4 id="h_01EVWYC3CAJY2C8P6DN522CZJG"><strong>1. Unaccredited certificate</strong></h4>
<p>Free online courses or training, such as <span style="font-family: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;">Certificate of Achievement, Certificate of Participation, or Certificate of Accomplishment.</span></p>
<p>Examples, courses from:</p>
<ul>
<li>Udemy, Coursera, or other MOOCs</li>
<li>Open2Study</li>
<li>ALISON online courses</li>
<li>Community-organized workshops</li>
</ul>

<p><span class="wysiwyg-font-size-small">Image from <a href="http://www.freeitonlinecourses.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/certificate-of-achievement-from-Open2Study.png" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FreeOnlineCourses.com</a></span></p>
<p> </p>
<h4><strong>2. Language proficiency certificate</strong></h4>
<p>This type of document shows your language proficiency, not your language teaching skills.</p>
<p>For example:</p>
<ul>
<li>IELTS, TOEFL (English proficiency exams)</li>
<li>DALF, DELF (French proficiency exams)</li>
<li>HSK (Chinese proficiency exam)</li>
</ul>
<p> </p>
<h3><strong>How does your resume appear on your profile?</strong></h3>
<p><strong>None of the documents will be published on your profile.</strong> Students can only see the description, not the original documents.</p>
