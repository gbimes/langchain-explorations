<h1>Ticketing Rules </h1>
<p> </p>
<h2><strong>1. Ticket Status: </strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>New:</strong> New ticket is a ticket that has not been touched or assigned. </li>
<li>
<strong>Open:</strong> <span>An </span>Open <span>ticket is defined as a ticket assigned to an agent.  Once a ticket's status has been changed from New to Open, it can never be set back to New. <strong>Tickets must be solved within 3 hours from the moment they are assigned. </strong></span><span></span>
</li>
<li>
<span><strong>Pending:</strong> Set ticket in "Pending" status when you need to ask for more information from the customer. If a ticket is in pending status and the user doesn't reply, we will resolve the ticket after 72 hrs. </span><span></span><span></span>
</li>
<li>
<span><strong>On-hold:</strong> Set ticket in "On-Hold" status when you need to wait for confirmation or perform any action from the company side. (<strong><span class="wysiwyg-underline">On-hold must be used together with schedule field!).</span></strong></span><span></span>
</li>
<li>
<strong>Solved: </strong>When you have answered all the questions and there are no further actions required from your side. </li>
</ul>
<p> </p>
<h2>
<strong>2. Ticket Rules:</strong><span></span>
</h2>
<p><strong>RULES:</strong></p>
<p><strong>1. Tickets must be taken from Tier 1/2 only sections.</strong></p>
<p><strong>2. Tickets must be taken accordingly to SLA* unless supervisor says otherwise.</strong></p>
<p><strong>3. Tickets must be taken one after the other = IN ORDER. After you take your tickets (between 5 min to 10 max) make sure that there's no missing taken ticket in the middle. </strong></p>
<p><strong>4. Cherry picking behaviour will be sanctioned. After the third (3rd) warning, italki has the right to end the contract. </strong></p>
<p> </p>
<p><strong>TICKETING SYSTEM:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<span>When escalating a ticket from Tier 1 to Tier 2, respond to the user, select ticket <strong>group(assignee)</strong>&amp;<strong>about</strong>, then submit as<strong> Open or Solved </strong>(<strong>depending on the Escalation Rules, </strong>see below). </span><span></span><span></span>
</li>
<li>
<span>When waiting for the user to provide more information, respond to the user, select ticket <strong>group</strong></span><span><strong>(assignee)</strong></span><span>&amp; <strong>about</strong>, then submit as <strong>Pending</strong>. </span><span></span><span></span>
</li>
<li>
<span>When waiting for italki to provide more information, respond to the user, select ticket <strong>group</strong></span><span><strong>(assignee)</strong></span>&amp; <strong style="font-size: 1.1em;">about</strong><span style="font-size: 1.1em;">, select "</span><strong style="font-size: 1.1em;">schedule</strong><span style="font-size: 1.1em;">" for how long you want to put it on hold, then submit as <strong>On-hold</strong>. *<strong>Please note the on-hold ticket will automatically open after the scheduled period. </strong></span>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.italki.com/hc/article_attachments/4856448654105/Screenshot_2022-03-15_at_10.56.47.png" alt="Screenshot_2022-03-15_at_10.56.47.png" width="288" height="681">                                       </p>
<ul>
<li>When the issue from the user has been addressed with no information required from either user or italki, submit the ticket as <strong>Solved</strong>. </li>
</ul>
