<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Here are 5 Keys to making great flashcards on italki: </span></h3>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">1. Pictures and mnemonics</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A key feature of flashcards is their ability to help students retain the information on the card. We can enhance this ability by creating cards with memorable pictures and mnemonics. </span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Even simple words can benefit from mnemonics, such as </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Friend: Fred rushed in eating nine doughnuts. What about a picture of Fred with nine doughnuts and his friends?</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">2. Examples </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Part of remembering vocabulary is the context and connection of words. Where and how a word is used can be just as important as pronunciation. For more subtle words providing an example sentence or other meanings to give context can help students understand and remember. </span></p>

Can you also make unique sets with different words that showcase your talents and specialties? The answer is also yes!  </span></p>

<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">4. Related not overlapped</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Building connections between words and phrases can help your students remember them. As such the words in your set should be related to a topic or theme, but try not to have words that overlap in meaning and use, as that can be confusing.</span></p>

<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">5. Focused </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Similar to being related, be focused. Keep your sets focused on a topic or word set. Help students stay focused by keeping the sets manageable and easy to review within 15 minutes. You don’t need to cover every word related to shopping in one set, maybe cover a certain type of store. </span></p>
