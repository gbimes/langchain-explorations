<h3><span style="color: #434343;">Lesson Overview</span></h3>
<p>This is all your data relating to lessons, let’s break it down. </p>
<p><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/Nz-uC_WZzCDATc8eT0EiKX8uzEopeH8dCqAw561Z4dplCq3_hMukidgFthKljQ9Nsi1m154G-xRt2qICVyVQ9TmVycXI5TSHltxr0V2aUWESv71cTOqPU0sR5gIgHDhiUZXnV_Xl9y7j2H_8Oz1zdgw">  </p>
<ul>
<li>“Total Lessons Requests” is <strong>the number of lesson requests you received</strong> in that month. </li>
<li>“Completed Lessons” is <strong>the number of lessons you completed</strong> that month. </li>
<li>“Expiration” is <strong>the number of lesson requests that expired without a response from you</strong> in that month. </li>
<li>“Decline” is <strong>the number of lesson requests that you declined</strong> in that month. </li>
<li>“Canceled” is <strong>the number of booked lessons you (not the student) canceled</strong> in that month.</li>
<li>“Absence” is <strong>the number of booked lessons that you were confirmed as absent</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<p>All of this is basic information about your lesson management and the actions you took on italki. These form the basis of student user experience on italki, and high-performing teachers look to minimize absences, cancelations, declines, and expirations.</p>
